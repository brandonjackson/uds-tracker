# Participatory Budgeting Platform

**Frontier Service 19**

Citizen proposal, deliberation, and allocation of public funds through digital platforms.

## Exemplars

### Brazil (Porto Alegre)

Digital evolution of the original participatory budgeting model. Porto Alegre pioneered participatory budgeting in 1989 and has progressively digitised the process, enabling broader citizen engagement in budget allocation decisions.

### Spain (Barcelona)

Decidim — an open-source participatory democracy platform now used by 150+ cities globally. Enables citizen proposals, deliberation, and voting on budget allocations and policy decisions.

### Taiwan

Join platform for public deliberation and policy consultation. Taiwan's digital democracy infrastructure enables citizens to propose, discuss, and influence policy at national scale. Combined with the vTaiwan process for consensus-building.

## Why This Matters

Participatory budgeting gives citizens direct influence over how public money is spent. Digital platforms remove barriers to participation and can scale deliberation from neighbourhood to national level.
