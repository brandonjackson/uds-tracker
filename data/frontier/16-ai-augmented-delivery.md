# AI-Augmented Public Service Delivery

**Frontier Service 16**

A family of AI tools that transform how citizens access government services and how public servants deliver them — spanning citizen-facing advisors, professional copilots, decision support, and admin automation.

## Exemplars

### United Kingdom (i.AI / GDS)

The most advanced programme globally:

- **GOV.UK AI Helpers** — an agentic AI companion (powered by Claude, via Anthropic partnership) piloting with 16–34 year olds for career guidance and apprenticeships in 2025/26, with ambition to scale to a unified citizen-facing service across all life events by 2026/27.
- **Caddy** — an AI copilot for Citizens Advice advisers, live in 31 offices with 70+ on the waiting list. In a randomised controlled trial, it halved response times and doubled adviser confidence.
- **Minute** — an AI scribe deployed to 1,000 officials in 22 local authorities. Forked by the Ministry of Justice as "Justice Transcribe" for probation officers.
- **Extract / Planning AI** — AI-assisted planning decisions in alpha with 7 councils, targeting all English councils by Spring 2026.
- **Lex** — semantic search over all UK legislation back to 1267, with an open-sourced "Parliament MCP." Over 700 active users.

### Estonia

Proactive services framework that automatically identifies citizen entitlements and initiates service delivery without requiring applications. The system detects life events and triggers relevant service delivery automatically.

## Why This Matters

The real innovation is not a single caseworker bot but a *family* of AI tools spanning the full service delivery chain. This reframing captures what's emerging and what other countries should aspire to replicate.
