# 11 - Electoral Services

## Tier
Essential

## What This Service Is
A government- or electoral-commission-operated digital system supporting the electoral process, including voter registration, voter roll management, candidate registration, results transmission, and publication of election results. This does not require electronic voting machines or internet voting, but focuses on the digital infrastructure surrounding elections.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Voters can check their registration status and polling station assignment online
- A digital voter registry exists that is used for voter roll management and deduplication
- Election results are transmitted, aggregated, and published electronically
- At least one citizen-facing electoral service (voter registration check, results lookup, or candidate information) is available digitally
- The system is operated by the national election management body

## What Does NOT Count
- A static website that posts PDF scans of final election results after manual tallying
- An internal voter database used only by election officials with no citizen-facing digital interface
- Electronic voting machines alone, without broader digital electoral administration infrastructure
- A social media page or mobile app that only provides election date reminders

## DPG Maturity Level
**Early** -- There are no widely adopted open-source digital public goods for electoral administration. Electoral systems are typically custom-built or provided by specialized vendors due to the high-security and high-trust requirements of the domain.
