# 02 - Civil Registration

## Tier
Foundation

## What This Service Is
A government system for the continuous, permanent, compulsory, and universal recording of vital events (births, deaths, marriages, divorces, and other civil status changes) occurring in a population. Digital civil registration means these events can be registered, stored, and retrieved electronically.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Vital events (at minimum births and deaths) can be registered through a digital system by authorized registrars or citizens
- Digital certificates or records can be issued, stored, and retrieved electronically
- The system covers at least the national level (not confined to a single municipality pilot)
- Data can be transmitted electronically between registration points and a central registry
- Notifications or integrations exist with at least one other government system (e.g., identity, health, population registry)

## What Does NOT Count
- A paper-based registry that is later digitized into a scanned archive without transactional capability
- A PDF birth certificate download from a website where the underlying registration is still manual
- A hospital-only electronic birth notification system with no link to the civil registry
- A pilot project covering fewer than one subnational administrative unit

## DPG Maturity Level
**Mature** -- OpenCRVS is deployed nationally in multiple countries. DHIS2 is used for vital statistics dashboards in several contexts. Interoperability standards for CRVS are well defined by UN agencies.
