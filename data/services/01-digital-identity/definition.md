# 01 - Digital Identity

## Tier
Foundation

## What This Service Is
A government-operated or government-recognized digital identity system that enables individuals to prove who they are when accessing public and private services online or in person. This includes foundational ID systems (national ID, unique identity number) and functional ID systems that are interoperable across government services.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Citizens can obtain a unique, verifiable digital identity credential issued or recognized by the government
- The identity can be authenticated electronically (e.g., biometric verification, QR code, smart card, or mobile-based authentication)
- At least one government service accepts the digital identity for online or in-person verification
- An API or protocol exists for third-party (public or private) service providers to verify identity with user consent
- The system covers a meaningful share of the eligible population (not just a pilot district)

## What Does NOT Count
- A PDF or paper-based national ID card with no electronic verification capability
- A username/password portal for a single ministry with no cross-service interoperability
- A biometric enrollment campaign with no mechanism to use the data for authentication
- Planned or legislated systems that have not yet issued credentials to any citizens

## DPG Maturity Level
**Mature** -- Multiple open-source platforms (MOSIP, OpenCRVS identity modules) are deployed at national scale in several countries. Standards such as OpenID Connect and ISO/IEC 19794 are well established.
