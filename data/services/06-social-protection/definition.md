# 06 - Social Protection

## Tier
Essential

## What This Service Is
A government-operated digital system for administering social protection programs, including social safety nets, cash transfers, food assistance, unemployment benefits, pensions, and disability support. This encompasses beneficiary registration, eligibility determination, payment disbursement, and grievance redressal.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Beneficiaries can register or be registered for social protection programs through a digital system
- Eligibility determination uses digital data (means testing, proxy means testing, or categorical criteria) processed electronically
- Payments or benefits are disbursed through digital channels (mobile money, bank transfer, digital voucher)
- A social registry or integrated beneficiary registry exists that is shared across at least two programs
- Beneficiaries have a digital channel for checking status, filing grievances, or updating information

## What Does NOT Count
- A paper-based beneficiary list digitized into a spreadsheet with no transactional capability
- Manual cash distribution where only the accounting is digital
- A single program's database with no interoperability with other social protection programs or government systems
- A website that only provides information about available programs with no registration or transaction capability

## DPG Maturity Level
**Emerging** -- OpenSPP and OpenG2P are relatively newer platforms gaining traction. The space has fewer mature open-source solutions compared to identity or health, but adoption is accelerating with support from international organizations.
