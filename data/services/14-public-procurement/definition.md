# 14 - Public Procurement

## Tier
Essential

## What This Service Is
A government-operated digital system for managing the public procurement process, including publication of tenders, submission of bids, evaluation, contract award, and contract management. This covers e-procurement portals, open contracting data platforms, and supplier registration systems.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Government tenders and procurement opportunities are published on a digital platform accessible to the public
- Suppliers can register and submit bids electronically
- Contract award decisions and, ideally, contract details are published digitally
- The system covers a significant share of public procurement (at minimum all central government procurement above a threshold)
- The platform provides structured, machine-readable data (not just PDF uploads of tender documents)

## What Does NOT Count
- A website that publishes PDF scans of tender notices from a newspaper without structured data or electronic bidding
- An internal procurement management system used by government officials with no public-facing portal
- A supplier directory or registration portal with no actual tendering or bidding functionality
- E-procurement for a single ministry or agency without a national or cross-government scope

## DPG Maturity Level
**Emerging** -- The Open Contracting Data Standard (OCDS) provides a widely adopted open standard for procurement data. Budeshi provides an open-source platform for procurement transparency. However, full end-to-end open-source e-procurement platforms with bid submission capability are less common.
