# 05 - Population Registry

## Tier
Foundation

## What This Service Is
A government-operated digital register that maintains a continuously updated record of all persons residing in the country (citizens, permanent residents, and in some cases temporary residents). The population registry serves as the authoritative source of demographic data and links to civil registration events, identity credentials, and addresses.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- A centralized or federated digital database exists containing demographic information (name, date of birth, sex, address, civil status) for the population
- Records are updated through links to civil registration (births, deaths, marriages) or administrative processes
- Authorized government agencies can query the registry electronically for service delivery or verification purposes
- The registry covers a substantial share of the resident population
- Data exchange interfaces (APIs or interoperability layers) exist for other government systems

## What Does NOT Count
- A one-time census database with no mechanism for continuous updates
- A voter roll maintained only for election purposes with no broader government use
- A spreadsheet or isolated database in a single ministry not connected to other systems
- A civil registration system that records events but does not maintain a current population stock register

## DPG Maturity Level
**Mature** -- MOSIP and OpenCRVS provide components applicable to population registries. Several countries have built national population registries on open-source stacks, though fully packaged open-source population registry solutions are less common than identity or CRVS platforms.
