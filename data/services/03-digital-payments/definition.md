# 03 - Digital Payments

## Tier
Foundation

## What This Service Is
A government-enabled or government-regulated digital payment infrastructure that allows individuals and businesses to send and receive money electronically. This includes real-time payment rails, mobile money interoperability layers, and government-to-person (G2P) payment systems.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- A national or widely adopted instant payment system or switch exists (e.g., real-time gross settlement for retail, mobile money interoperability)
- Citizens can make person-to-person (P2P) or person-to-government (P2G) payments electronically
- Government can disburse payments (salaries, subsidies, social transfers) digitally to citizen accounts
- The system supports interoperability across at least two financial service providers (banks, mobile money operators, or payment service providers)
- The infrastructure operates at national scale, not limited to a single corridor or pilot

## What Does NOT Count
- A single mobile money operator with no interoperability with other providers or government systems
- A bank transfer system limited to large-value interbank settlement with no retail or citizen-facing access
- A government portal that accepts credit card payments through a private international gateway with no domestic payment rail
- Planned payment switch legislation with no operational system

## DPG Maturity Level
**Mature** -- Mojaloop is deployed or in advanced implementation in multiple countries. Open standards for payment interoperability (ISO 20022, Open Banking APIs) are well established. GNU Taler provides a privacy-preserving electronic payment alternative.
