# 14 - Immigration and Travel

## Tier
Essential

## What This Service Is
A government-operated digital system for managing immigration services including visa applications, border control, passport issuance, residence permits, work permits, and travel authorization. This covers both the citizen/traveler-facing application systems and the backend border management infrastructure.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- At least one major immigration service (visa application, passport application/renewal, or travel authorization) can be initiated or completed online
- An electronic border management system exists for processing arrivals and departures
- Travelers or applicants can check application status digitally
- Machine-readable travel documents (e-passports or electronic visa stamps) are issued
- The system operates at the national level across all major ports of entry

## What Does NOT Count
- A website that provides visa requirements information and downloadable application forms that must be submitted at an embassy in person
- Machine-readable passports alone (the document is digital but the issuance process may not be)
- An airline-operated advance passenger information system with no government-facing digital immigration service
- A border management system that only records entries/exits in a logbook later digitized

## DPG Maturity Level
**Early** -- There are no widely adopted open-source DPGs for immigration and travel management. The sector relies heavily on proprietary systems from specialized vendors (e.g., border management solutions from international organizations like IOM, or commercial providers). High-security requirements and international interoperability standards (ICAO) shape the landscape.
