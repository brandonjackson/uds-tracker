# 08 - Land and Property Registry

## Tier
Essential

## What This Service Is
A government-operated digital system for registering, recording, and managing land ownership, tenure rights, property boundaries, and property transactions. This includes cadastral systems (mapping land parcels), title registries (recording ownership), and transaction systems (recording transfers, mortgages, and encumbrances).

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Land parcels or property records are stored in a digital database (cadastral and/or title registry)
- Property owners or authorized parties can search or verify land records electronically
- At least some land transactions (transfers, title searches, or certificate requests) can be initiated or completed digitally
- The system covers at least one major urban area or a significant portion of the national territory
- Digital maps or spatial data are linked to ownership records

## What Does NOT Count
- A scanned archive of paper title deeds with no searchable database or transactional capability
- A GIS mapping project that maps parcels but has no link to ownership or legal records
- An internal-only database accessible to registry staff but not to the public or other agencies
- A pilot project covering a single community or test area with no plan for scale

## DPG Maturity Level
**Emerging** -- SOLA (Solutions for Open Land Administration) and Cadasta provide open-source tools for land administration, but national-scale adoption remains limited. The sector is characterized by high customization needs and complex legal frameworks that slow standardization.
