# 12 - Justice and Legal Aid

## Tier
Essential

## What This Service Is
A government-operated digital system for accessing justice services, including case management, court filing, legal information, legal aid applications, and dispute resolution. This covers both the court administration infrastructure and citizen-facing access-to-justice tools.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Citizens can access legal information (rights, procedures, available services) through a government digital platform
- At least one of the following is available digitally: court case filing, case status tracking, legal aid application, or online dispute resolution
- Court case management is handled through a digital system (even if not fully citizen-facing)
- The system is operated or endorsed by the judiciary, ministry of justice, or legal aid authority
- The service is available at the national level or across a significant jurisdiction

## What Does NOT Count
- A ministry of justice website that only publishes legislation and organizational information
- A legal information chatbot or app operated by a private NGO with no government integration
- Digitized court archives (scanned historical records) with no active case management or filing capability
- A video conferencing tool used for remote hearings without broader digital case management

## DPG Maturity Level
**Early** -- There are no widely adopted open-source DPGs for justice and legal aid administration. Court management systems are typically proprietary or custom-built. Some open data initiatives exist (e.g., open court data standards) but full-stack justice platforms remain a gap.
