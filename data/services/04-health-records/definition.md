# 04 - Health Records

## Tier
Foundation

## What This Service Is
A government-operated or government-mandated digital system for recording, storing, and exchanging individual health information across healthcare facilities and providers. This includes electronic health records (EHR), health information exchanges (HIE), and shared health data platforms used for clinical care and public health.

## What Counts as "Digitally Available"
- Individual patient health data (encounters, diagnoses, prescriptions, immunizations) is recorded and stored electronically
- Health records are accessible to authorized providers across at least two facilities or administrative levels
- A health information exchange or shared data layer exists enabling data flow between systems
- Citizens or patients can access at least some of their health information digitally (e.g., vaccination records, lab results)
- The system operates at subnational or national scale, beyond a single hospital or clinic

## What Does NOT Count
- A hospital information system confined to a single facility with no data exchange capability
- A paper-based patient record system with a scanned document archive
- A public health dashboard (e.g., disease surveillance) with no individual-level patient records
- A mobile health app that stores data only on the patient's device with no facility integration

## DPG Maturity Level
**Mature** -- DHIS2 is the world's most widely deployed health information platform. OpenMRS provides EHR capability in many low-resource settings. OpenHIE defines an interoperability architecture adopted by multiple countries.
